Glossary

ALGORITHM

Set of operational rules whose application allows solving a problem stated through a finite number of operations.

COMPUTATIONAL BIOLOGY

Branch of biology that involves the development and application of data analysis methods, theoretical approaches, mathematical modeling and computational simulation techniques to study biological systems.

COMPUTATION

Way of calculating time; method used to calculate a date or duration.

CRYSTALLOGRAPHY

Science of crystallized matter, laws presiding over its formation, its structure, its geometric, physical and chemical properties.

MATILDA EFFECT

Phenomenon designating the recurring denial or minimization of women scientists' contributions in favor of their male colleagues.

NUCLEAR FISSION

Division of a heavy atomic nucleus, generally into two parts, called fission products, with energy release and neutron emission.

ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE

Artificial intelligence aims to reproduce as closely as possible, using machines, mental activities, whether they are of the order of understanding, perception, or decision-making.

ANALYTICAL ENGINE

Mechanical machine designed by Charles Babbage in the 19th century, ancestor of the modern computer, capable of performing complex calculations.

NEUROSCIENCE

Set of disciplines studying the nervous system (neurobiology, neurochemistry, neurohistology, neuroanatomy, neuropharmacology, neuropsychology and neurolinguistics, neuropathology, neurology, psychiatry, neuroendocrinology, neurosurgery).

NEURON

Basic cell of nervous tissue, capable of receiving, analyzing and producing information. The main part, or cell body of the neuron, is equipped with extensions, dendrites and the axon.

NEUROTRANSMITTER

Chemical mediator synthesized and released by a neuron, allowing it to transmit messages by binding to other cells.

ACTION POTENTIAL

Unidirectional electrical signal traveling through neuronal axons, which causes the release of neurotransmitters at synapses. Also called nerve impulse.

PROGRAMMING

Set of activities related to the definition, writing, debugging and execution of computer programs; sequence of orders to which a device must obey.
