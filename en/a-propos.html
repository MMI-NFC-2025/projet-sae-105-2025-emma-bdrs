About Me

My name is Emma Badarous, 18 years old, student in MMI (multimedia and internet professions)
For my website project, I explore the Matilda effect, a phenomenon that denounces the invisibilization of women in scientific discoveries.
My guiding thread is Ada Lovelace, programming pioneer and muse of algorithmic thought.
Through this site, I want to revive the forgotten legacy of women in science and highlight the modernity of Ada Lovelace, in a journey between art, technology and memory, where design connects past and future.

Project Presentation

The project consists of creating a mobile-first website dedicated to Ada Lovelace, an emblematic figure victim of the Matilda effect.

The site, available in French and English, will offer a home page presenting articles on Ada Lovelace, women of science in literature, popularization of her theory, rehabilitation of female figures through series, as well as an object related to the theme from the University Library.

Other content will enrich the site: a timeline of forgotten women scientists, a film gallery, series excerpts and a contact form.

The objective is to design a site that is both aesthetic, educational and eco-responsible, aiming for an "A" score on ecoindex.fr, to value the memory of women scientists in a modern and sustainable approach.
